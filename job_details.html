What You'll Do

  • Provide knowledgeable and caring support to family and friends of deceased; meets with family and/or friends of the deceased to discuss the nature and time of funeral arrangements to be conducted.
  • Tactfully discuss the options and preference for disposition of the remains; address and explain the costs of the funeral with family and/or friends of the deceased.
  • Coordinate practical arrangements for the funeral that are respectful, professional, timely, and sensitive.
  • Coordinate the transfer of the body or remains to the funeral home.
  • Provide instructions to the Funeral Arrangements Supervisor and Funeral Attendant regarding times, rooms, and arrangements to be made.
  • Schedules clergy and pallbearers, answering any questions about their specific duties.

Required Skills

  • Ability to manage distressing and stressful situations with sensitivity and care.
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills.
  • Thorough understanding of methods for comforting people during grief.
  • Ability to promptly and accurately execute required legal paperwork.
  • Detail-oriented and extremely organized.
  • Ability to perform mortuary services.
